BEHAVIOUR AND TRAINING

Understand Cat Behaviour

  1. Body Language:
    • Tail: A high tail shows happiness and confidence, while a low or tucked tail indicates fear or anxiety.
    • Ears: Forward-facing ears show interest, while flattened ears signal fear or aggression.
    • Purring: Usually means contentment, but can also indicate distress.
    • Kneading: A sign of comfort and pleasure from kittenhood.
  2. Common Behaviors:
    • Scratching: Cats scratch to mark territory, stretch, and shed old claw sheaths. Provide scratching posts to protect your furniture.
    • Litter Box Use: Cats are clean animals, but changes in litter box habits can indicate health issues or stress.
    • Play: Essential for physical and mental stimulation. Use interactive toys and play sessions to prevent boredom and behavioral issues.

Training Tips

  1. Positive Reinforcement:
    • Reward desired behaviors with treats, praise, and affection.
    • Avoid punishment, as it can lead to fear and aggression.
  2. Clicker Training:
    • Use a clicker to mark desired behaviors, followed by a reward.
    • Start with simple commands like "sit" or "come," associating the click with the reward.
  3. Litter Box Training:
    • Place the litter box in a quiet, accessible location.
    • Keep it clean to encourage use.
    • Clean accidents thoroughly to remove the scent and prevent repeats.
  4. Discouraging Unwanted Behavior:
    • Use deterrents like double-sided tape or citrus sprays to keep cats away from certain areas.
    • Redirect scratching to scratching posts by placing them in your cat's favorite spots.
  5. Socialization:
    • Gradually introduce your cat to new people, animals, and environments to build confidence.
    • Use positive reinforcement to make new experiences enjoyable.

Addressing Behavioral Issues

  1. Aggression:
    • Identify and avoid triggers that provoke aggression.
    • Provide plenty of playtime to burn off excess energy.
  2. Anxiety:
    • Create a safe, quiet space for your cat to retreat to.
    • Use calming products like pheromone diffusers to reduce stress.
  3. Destructive Behavior:
    • Provide lots of toys and activities to prevent boredom.
    • Rotate toys to keep them interesting.

Health and Well-being

  1. Regular Vet Visits:
    • Regular check-ups can catch health issues early, which can affect behavior.
    • Keep vaccinations and parasite control up to date.
  2. Diet and Nutrition:
    • Provide a balanced diet suitable for your cat’s age and health needs.
    • Monitor your cat’s weight and avoid overfeeding.
  3. Environmental Enrichment:
    • Offer climbing structures, hiding places, and interactive toys for mental and physical stimulation.
    • Consider window perches for bird watching and safe outdoor exploration.
